What you can do

Pick a site on the map

The landing is a national map of every NEON site — dot size is species richness, colour is the biome (forest, prairie, desert, tundra, tropical dry forest). Tap a dot to dive in.

Across the continent

The flagship: every site as a dot in climate space — breeding-season temperature against its bird community, with richness rarefied to equal survey effort so it reflects ecology, not effort. A continental temperature–productivity gradient, stated honestly as space-for-time.

The Bird Board

Every species on one board — ubiquity (how widespread) × detection index (how often counted), coloured by how it's detected: singing, calling, or seen. Tap a dot to pin its card.

Species profiles

Open any species for its detectability-by-distance signature (area-corrected), yearly counts, and a downloadable card + raw detections (CSV).

When the counts happen

For each site, the breeding-count window shaded against its green-up and temperature year — co-located NEON environment data, as honest seasonal context (not a fabricated bird-vs-weather link).

Tap a grid for its bird list

On the site map, click any point-count grid for the full list of birds detected there — sorted, with detections and counts — and download it as CSV.

Honest statistics

Every view states its method, on the chart:

  • Detection index, not population — a loud species and a quiet one at equal density give unequal counts, so the abundance axis is birds per 6-minute point-count, never a census. Ubiquity (% of points detected) is the less count-biased axis, though still a detection floor.
  • Richness done right — Chao2 and species accumulation use the point × year occasion as the incidence unit (a point's yearly revisits aren't pooled as separate places). Across sites, richness is rarefied to a common number of point-counts, because raw richness just tracks effort.
  • Detectability — each species' detections-by-distance are area-corrected to detections per hectare, since a raw point-count histogram rises with distance on geometry alone.
  • Climate gradient — a space-for-time comparison of 46 contemporaneous places, correlational and confounded by biome and latitude — never read as one site warming. Precipitation is shown only for the 19 sites with a NEON gauge, never imputed.
