# Breeding landbird point counts — Expert Review by Wren (NEON DP1.10003.001)
_Devoted product-expert review — June 2026._

> I read this app end to end against the NEON DP1.10003.001 SOP and the distance-sampling / occupancy literature, and I'll say it plainly: **this is the most honest avian point-count explorer I have reviewed, full stop.** It refuses the one lie that sinks most bird dashboards — it never calls a point-count total a population. It labels the abundance axis a *detection index* on every surface (`ui.R:62`, `server.R:88`, `server.R:120`), it de-pseudoreplicates onto the point×year occasion (`bird_helpers.R:61`), it takes effort from the structural `brd_perpoint` table and not the detection-row count (`server.R:47`, guarding a ~92× error), and it recoded the `observerDistance == 999` "not estimable" sentinel to NA at build time — the single most important data-hygiene step for this product. That fix is real and verified: 1,199 sentinel records gone, `visualfar` false-positives down 1,304→702, and zero headline numbers moved (`docs/DATA-TAKEAWAYS.md`). What remains is not a list of errors — it's a short list of places where the app under-hedges a still-defensible number, and four product-native signals it computes but doesn't fully surface. None of it touches the math; all of it touches honesty-at-the-margin. Lead with temp→richness pooled across 46 sites, put a CI on it, stop leading the demo with a 3× Chao2 extrapolation, and quarantine the flyovers — and this is journal- and agency-defensible as it stands.

## Method fidelity (is the NEON protocol represented correctly?)

Faithful, and described in the right words.

- **The protocol is named and framed correctly.** 6-minute count, observer records every bird by sight or sound, distance to each, 1–2× per breeding season, same points re-surveyed yearly, 46 sites (`ui.R:53`, `server.R:86`, `about` panel `server.R:442`). This is the variable-circular-plot / unlimited-radius design (Reynolds et al. 1980; Ralph et al. 1995), and the app's distance machinery treats it as distance-sampling-ready rather than a fixed-radius index — which is exactly what `observerDistance` is *for*.
- **The occasion unit is correct and load-bearing.** `sampling_occasion()` = `paste(sp$pointkey, sp$year)` (`bird_helpers.R:61`); Chao2, accumulation, and rarefaction all run on it. Pooling a point's yearly revisits as distinct places is the cardinal pseudoreplication error for this product, and the app avoids it everywhere. This is the first thing I check on any avian richness code, and it passes.
- **The effort denominator is the structural table, not the detection rows.** `rv$nvis` comes from `b$meta$n_visits` / `sum(points$n_visits)`, with an explicit comment refusing the `n_distinct(obs$eventID)` fallback (`server.R:43-47`). The grains differ ~92× at HARV (646 point-visits vs 7 eventIDs, `DATA-TAKEAWAYS.md`); using the wrong one inflates every per-effort index by that factor. Correct, and the failure mode is fail-loud (NA), not silently-wrong.
- **The `999` sentinel is recoded at BUILD, not just flagged.** `na_sentinel()` in `scripts/bundle_bird_data.R` recodes `999`/`9999`→NA, with a comment that names the cause (flocks / flyovers / detected-but-far). This is the right altitude — fix it once, upstream, so it routes into honest missing-distance accounting (`bird_helpers.R:239`) instead of polluting distance medians and tripping the `visualfar` flag (602 visual records were being mislabelled as real long-range visual IDs). I would carry this verbatim to every distance-bearing NEON product.
- **Morphospecies are dropped before any *species* richness.** `species_level_only()` (`bird_helpers.R:14`) gates on `is_species` / `taxonRank`, so "Icteridae sp." and slash entries don't inflate Chao2 or the board. Correct.
- **Distance is area-corrected.** `distance_decay()` (`bird_helpers.R:136`) divides each annulus count by its area (`area ∝ 2π·r·Δr`), truncates at 200 m per Buckland practice, and n-gates at 8 detections. A raw point-count distance histogram *rises* then falls on geometry alone; this recovers the monotone detectability signature. It correctly stops short of fitting g(d) and calling it density — the right call given observer-estimated, coarse distances and thin per-species counts.

One small fidelity note, not an error: the app describes the count window as "once or twice each breeding season" (`server.R:442`), which is right for the *occasion*, but the science point that matters downstream is that this cadence means **there is no within-season bird time series to lag** — and the app already says exactly that in the seasonal panel (`server.R:435`). Good.

## Analysis & metrics — defensible? (with the literature)

Defensible to a journal or agency reviewer, with two hedges I'd add before submission.

- **Detection index (`index = total_birds / n_visits`, `bird_helpers.R:45`).** This is `count / effort` — a relative index, fine as long as it's never labelled abundance, and it never is. The confound it can't escape (detection = detectability × abundance; a loud territorial ovenbird and a quiet flycatcher at equal density give unequal counts; Buckland et al. 2001; Royle 2004) is stated on the hero (`server.R:88`), the overview caveat (`ui.R:62`), and the board (`server.R:166`). This is the (a)-path of the only two honest choices — *label it an index* or *model p explicitly* — and given the data depth here, (a) is the correct, defensible choice. The honest upgrade past it is occupancy ψ (MacKenzie et al. 2002), which NEON's yearly revisits *can* support as a multi-season model; that's a future feature, not a fix.
- **Ubiquity / naïve occupancy (`bird_helpers.R:44`).** Fraction of points ever detected — a *floor*, biased low for quiet/secretive species, still effort-dependent. The About panel says exactly this: "treat it as a floor, not detection-corrected occupancy" (`server.R:445`). Correct framing; this is the literature's standard caveat and it's stated.
- **Chao2 (`chao2_points()`, `bird_helpers.R:68`).** The math is right (Chao 1987; the `(m-1)/m` finite-sample correction and the `Q2=0` fallback are both correct). The `unstable = Q2 < 3` flag is the right diagnostic. **My one statistical objection: it is reported as a bare point estimate with no interval.** At the demo site HARV, that's "Chao2 297" from S_obs 97 with **Q2 = 1** — a 3.06× extrapolation, the single most extrapolation-sensitive estimate in the entire 46-site dataset, and *the first number a new user meets* (`server.R:140`). The banner does append "(a rough floor)" when unstable, but a 3× extrapolation printed as a clean integer invites false precision. Chao2 has a known log-normal analytic variance (Chao 1987). **Fix:** suppress the point estimate when `Q2 < 3` and lead with `coverage` (0.99 here — the honest completeness story, already computed in `coverage_incidence()`, `bird_helpers.R:113`), or print the analytic 95% CI alongside. This is the one place the app genuinely under-hedges.
- **The cross-site gradient (`server.R:386`).** Spearman ρ = 0.39, breeding_temp_c vs S_rare, p ≈ 0.007, n = 46 (`DATA-TAKEAWAYS.md`). The richness axis is correctly **rarefied to t = 99 occasions** (`rarefy_incidence`, Colwell et al. 2012) — non-negotiable, because raw S_obs is an effort map that re-orders completely under rarefaction (UNDE/KONZ fall from rank ~4.5 to ~18–19; CPER 66→33.4). The space-for-time caveat is on the chart, with the biome/latitude confound named (`server.R:394`). **But the ρ annotation carries no CI, no p, and no n.** With 46 sites a Fisher-z or bootstrap interval is cheap and would stop a moderate correlation being over-read as a warming forecast. **Fix:** annotate "ρ = 0.39, 95% CI [0.11, 0.61], n = 46" instead of the bare ρ. (Note the live `stats::cor()` recomputes ρ as the user switches metric/unit — good, the number always matches the dots — so the CI must be computed live too.)
- **The literature placement is correct.** NEON's VCP design is closest to IMBCR (Pavlacky et al. 2017), which carries the distance information BBS historically lacked; these data feed continental status-and-trend work (Rosenberg et al. 2019; Partners in Flight). The app's cross-continent temp→richness analysis is a legitimate space-for-time community comparison — *correlational*, and it says so. No overclaim.

## What the field would add (collection / analysis / presentation / use)

Four product-native signals the app already computes or nearly computes, and one collection-side note:

1. **Quarantine the flyovers from a *breeding* index.** `species_board()` sums **all** `clusterSize` regardless of `detectionMethod` (`bird_helpers.R:38`) — so 3,104 `flyover` detections, including 300/150/130-bird Franklin's Gull flocks at CLBJ and a 500-bird White-winged Dove flock at LAJA, pump the "birds per count" detection index and `birds_per_count`. A flyover is not a breeding territory; a continental landbird-monitoring reviewer (IMBCR/BBS convention) would expect flyovers and non-breeding flocks either excluded from a breeding-density index or flagged. **Fix:** exclude `flyover` from the index (or offer a toggle) and state it; the math is a one-line filter and it would make the index meaningfully less flock-distorted. I confirmed the bundler does *not* do this today — its only `flyover` reference is the 999-sentinel comment.

2. **Map compound detection methods to their dominant component.** `method_col()` (`bird_helpers.R:23`) dumps `calling and singing` (3,908 detections) and `visual and singing` (1,337) into grey "other," discarding real signal and miscolouring the board and overview bars. **Fix:** map `*and singing` → singing, `calling and *` → calling, etc., before colouring. A singing component *is* a territorial-breeding signal and should read green, not grey.

3. **Promote `pct_singing` to a first-class community axis.** Singing share runs ~28% (BARR, treeless tundra) to ~80% (forest) — a genuine habitat/detectability signature already computed in `cross_site.rds`, but only surfaced as a one-line footnote in the overview prose (`server.R:118`). It's a defensible secondary y-metric on the gradient tab (it's *not* in the `gradMetric` choices, `ui.R:128`). **Fix:** add "Singing share" to the metric selector — it's free and it's honest.

4. **Ship a machine-readable codebook.** The CSV exports (`spCsv`, `gridSpeciesCsv`, `qcReportCsv`) are tidy and well-named, but **no `codebook.csv` / `data_dictionary.md` ships** (verified — none exists in the repo). A downstream user gets a column called `observerDistance` with NA values and no statement that NA means "distance not estimable" (the former 999 sentinel), nor the `index`/`ubiquity` formulas, nor the `clusterSize`/`detectionMethod` semantics. **Fix:** ship a column dictionary and a download button; the README "Data" section is the seed. This is the one FAIR gap.

5. **Collection-side, for the record (not the app's fault):** `observerDistance` is observer-*estimated*, not rangefinder-measured, so the distance decay is a coarse detectability sketch, not a calibrated detection function — which is precisely why the app stops short of density. Correct restraint. If NEON ever ships laser-validated distances or a double-observer subsample, this product could support actual distance-sampling density at the better-sampled forest sites; today it cannot, and the app honestly doesn't pretend.

## Product-specific honesty & QC traps

The traps I check this product for, and how the app handles each:

- **The `999`/`9999` sentinel — HANDLED (build-time recode).** The hardest avian-distance trap on NEON data: 999 is finite and >500, so it sits *below* the ">1 km implausible" flag while *tripping* the "visual-at-long-range" flag, silently mislabelling 602 flyover/flock records as real long-range visual IDs. Recoded to NA at build (`na_sentinel()`), bundles migrated (`scripts/recode_sentinel_distance.R`), 0 sentinels remain, `visualfar` candidates 1,304→702, no headline moved. This is exactly right, and it's the trap most apps miss.
- **Flock distortion of the index — PARTIALLY handled.** Labelled everywhere as detection-index-not-population (good), but flyovers/flocks are still *summed into* the index (see "What the field would add" #1). The label is honest; the metric could be cleaner.
- **"Not detected ≠ absent" — HANDLED.** Stated on the species hero (`server.R:84`), the Chao2 framing, and the about panel. Ubiquity is called a floor. Correct.
- **Raw richness is an effort map — HANDLED.** The gradient rarefies by default; observed richness is offered but labelled "effort differs" (`server.R:356`) and the confound string adds survey-effort to the caveat when observed is selected (`server.R:387`). Correct.
- **Chao2 instability at Q2<3 — UNDER-HEDGED.** See Analysis section; the demo leads with the least-stable estimate in the dataset. This is the one honesty gap that actually shows a number I'd want softened.
- **The `bird_qc()` system (`bird_helpers.R:188`) — GOLD STANDARD.** Ranked "verify, not wrong" flags + the exact offending rows + clickable inspector + per-flag and full-report CSV, with a green clean-path. Thresholds are BBS/IMBCR/distance-sampling-grounded (>1 km = high; visual >500 m = warn; exact-0 m heaping = warn; solitary-species mega-cluster via p99≤3 vs flocking-species log-MAD outlier — a genuinely thoughtful split that keeps Red-winged Blackbird's real flock tail out of the "error" branch; ≥25% missing-distance = info; under-effort points via within-site MAD). Flagged records are KEPT for modelling, truncated only at analysis (Buckland). This is the feature the rest of the suite should copy verbatim, and it does.

## Place in the suite / cascade

This app is the **consumer (bird) rung's corroboration layer**, and it knows its limits. Per project memory and the SOP cadence, **birds cannot carry a sub-annual lag** — point counts run only 1–2× per breeding season, so there is no within-season bird time series to correlate against climate or plants. The app encodes this honestly: the seasonal panel is explicitly *context, not a driver model* (`server.R:435`). The one signal it contributes *upward* is its own clean read of the suite's headline gradient — **breeding-season temperature → bird richness, ρ ≈ 0.39 pooled across 46 sites** — and the cascade must pull the **rarefied** `S_rare` @ t=99 as the bird community metric, **never** the flock-dominated detection index and **never** raw observed richness. It is the corroborating echo of the cascade's one robust link (temperature→green-up), one rung further down (climate→plants→consumers), confounded by biome and latitude, and honest only when pooled — a per-site read at n≈6–12 occasions is a false-negative regime. This explorer is also the suite's **honesty exemplar**: its detection-index / space-for-time / rarefied-by-default framing should be reused verbatim wherever the cascade speaks about consumers.
